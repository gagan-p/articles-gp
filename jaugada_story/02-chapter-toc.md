## Chapter Descriptions

### Ch 1 — Why Here?
*Location: Pandia/Jaugada*

The family drives from Berhampur to Jaugada. Aman expects a boulder in a
village and finds instead a three-kilometre fort with pre-Mauryan coins.
The question shifts from "why would Ashoka put something here" to "what was
already here that Ashoka found." Nrusingha at the site establishes the
corridor logic: this is a transit point, not a production centre. Aman
searches for the king of Kalinga's name and finds nothing. The notebook opens.

---

### Ch 2 — The Five Stones and the House That Burned
*Location: Jaugada compound*

The Pandava stones under the banyan tree — tended by women for generations
without institutional support, while the edict needed an iron cage. Minati's
grandmother came here in a line of white-saree women from Surada; this is the
first appearance of the women's traditions thread. The lac fort etymology
(Jatudurga) connects to the Lakshagriha of the Mahabharata. The chapter
closes on the drive to Bada Agula, the question of which came first —
the name or the story — left open.

---

### Ch 3 — The Edict That Isn't There
*Location: Jaugada + dhaba near Purushottampur*

Achintya arrives. He stands at the Pandava stones first, per Aman's
instruction. The structural analysis: Edict XIII (the confession, the
casualty numbers, the remorse) is absent from both Kalinga sites and present
at fourteen sites elsewhere. The Separate Kalinga Edicts confirm the frontier
reading in the edict's own language. The Rajavacanika title is unique to
Jaugada. At the dhaba, Achintya introduces the Kalinga Jina — two words in
the notebook, underlined twice, not opened again that night.

---

### Ch 4 — The House of the Key
*Location: Bada Agula; Berhampur (Nabin's house)*

The ancestral concrete house, the borrowed wire, the woman from two houses
down. Gagan describes what is now land: the kanchi ghar, the cattle, the
amba ghar, the khani. Nabin arrives, corrects the cattle count, adds the
amba ghar roof detail, gives the khani protocol and the sacrificial layer.
Nanda at the Alekh Akhada — one point, one visit. Sona ma — one song.
The Jatra at night. In Nabin's Berhampur house, Padmini's shelf holds
Satyabadi's book — Aman notices it, is told briefly who Satyabadi was.

---

### Ch 5 — Athagada
*Location: Athagada Patna, Kabisuryanagar*

The 16th century Jagannath temple in the hills. The temple priest tells the
Kalapahada story — idol hidden, recovered — without drama, the way you tell
something everyone knows. Achintya on Kabisurya Baladev Rath: writing Odia
when Odia was being squeezed out of official use. The hill country to the
west is visible: the same terrain the Samapa edict called "unconquered
territories." Achintya tells Aman that Bada Agula was in the Athagada
zamindari — the unidentified keys in the kanchi ghar box acquire a possible address.

---

### Ch 6 — Bhanjanagar
*Location: Bhanjanagar, Minati's parents' house*

Minati's parents' house on the Rushikulya. Aman puts his hand in the river
for the first time at volume — cold, fast, real. Minati's father mentions the
Ghumusar wars over dinner the way you mention weather. Minati's mother tells
the Tapoi story — the woman left behind when the Sadhaba brothers sail,
tending the house and the tulsi. Aman connects: the woman from two houses
down in Bada Agula is the Tapoi function without knowing the name of what
she is doing. Achintya confirms Bali Yatra is November 7th.

---

### Ch 7 — Upstream
*Location: Rushikulya valley; Rushimala spring*
*Contains Dream 1*

The drive up the valley with Minati's parents following partway. In the car
the lac connection is made: Jaugada named for lac, lac sealed the amphorae,
lac was the trade good, the fort controlled the lac trade. Merchant republic
hypothesis stated, structural weakness identified. The road fails; they walk.
The spring: a crack in granite, thirty centimetres wide, undramatic. Aman
sits on warm granite as the light goes. Dream 1: the Palur beach, Bindusara's
son has the throne, lac sacks, kings come and go. He wakes to Gagan's torch.

---

### Ch 8 — The Drunk Man
*Location: Forest path down from Rushimala; Forest Dept rest house*

The walk back down in the dark. The torch finds a man at the third granite
outcrop, mid-monologue about someone named Gobinda, and inside the Gobinda
grievance: both river-origin myths delivered in one sentence without knowing
they are being delivered. The penance version and the grief/tears version.
"The water doesn't write things down. That's why it gets there and you don't."
At the rest house, Aman writes up the dream before it dissolves, then the
drunk man's two stories — notes them, does not interpret.

---

### Ch 9 — What the Theory Weighs
*Location: Drive down to coast; Chatrapur; Rushikulya mouth*

Aman states the full theory to Achintya by phone on the drive down: seven
minutes. Achintya identifies four weaknesses; the structural weakness that
matters most is that Ashoka never names enemies anywhere, so the missing
Kalingan king may be rhetorical convention, not evidence of political
structure. The theory is revised: the trade corridor reading stands without
the merchant republic hypothesis. Chatrapur noted as the third location of
the British Collectorate in sixty years. The Rushikulya mouth: Aman looks
southeast toward Sri Lanka.

---

### Ch 10 — What the Excavation Found
*Location: Palur excavation site; Potagarh*

The 2023 OIMSEAS excavation: the Buddhist stupa on the hilltop (1st-2nd
century CE), rouletted ware, amphora pieces, clay bullae with Roman Silenus
head imagery. Aman picks up a rouletted ware shard — Prasanna stops him —
sets it back. The weight is the same as the dream shard. Aman's hypothesis:
the stupa as commercial signal. Achintya: it post-dates Ashoka; the signal
may not have been physical. Potagarh: the buried fort, the old Masjid, the
caretaker's shelf, the copper disc — Jain kayotsarga figure, possibly early
historic. The shelf is the provenance. NOT claimed as Kalinga Jina.

---

### Ch 11 — Twenty-Two Zamindaris and a Bag
*Location: Potagarh beach; Brahmapur*

The net man on Potagarh beach tells the Vijaya oral tradition: a prince sent
away, something sacred taken, connected to his dishonour. Aman writes: two
stories that rhyme, rhyming is not evidence. Drive to Brahmapur. Achintya
outlines the twenty-two Ganjam zamindaris. Subrat's apartment. The tin trunk.
Pitambara's papers, 1924-1936: the 1934 Jaugada draft arguing Ganjam is
ancestrally Odia because the edict marks Kalinga's southern boundary. Nabin
reads the last sheet (1 April 1936) alone. Puts it back. Not tonight.

---

### Ch 12 — The River of Compassion
*Location: Dhauli*
*Contains Dream 2*

First time leaving Ganjam district. The drive north on NH16, Chilika in
passing. Dhauli: the rock-cut elephant, the Shanti Stupa (1972, Japanese),
the Daya river below. The battlefield with no battle — no archaeological
evidence of mass combat. The logistics of 150,000 deportees: where did they
go? The Dhauli/Jaugada hierarchy confirmed: Dhauli got the prince-viceroy,
Jaugada got the middle managers. Dream 2: the Mahamatra of Samapa reads the
edict the way a middle manager reads a company values statement.

---

### Ch 13 — The City With No Burnt Layer
*Location: Sisupalgarh; Khandagiri/Udayagiri*
*Contains Dream 3*

Sisupalgarh: the octagonal fort, no destruction layer, continuous occupation
6th century BCE to 4th century CE. The same walls served the Mauryans and
Kharavela. Minati's dental analogy: no fracture doesn't mean no trauma, but
it tells you about the tooth's resilience. Khandagiri: the Hathigumpha, a
Jain pilgrim passing says something containing "Kalinga Jina" without
explanation, keeps walking. Second mention. Dream 3: the British surveyor of
the 1870s explains with total unselfconsciousness why the Raj needed a good
Indian emperor.

---

### Ch 14 — Bali Yatra
*Location: Cuttack, Mahanadi ghat; road between Bhubaneswar and Cuttack*
*Contains Dream 4*

November 7th, Kartik Purnima. On the road to Cuttack, a figure on the verge
— possibly an Ajivika, possibly a dream. The Mahanadi at Cuttack: the paper
boats, the mustard oil lamps, the old women selling boats for twenty rupees.
Minati buys three. Aman sets his on the water without writing anything.
"Everything I know. There wasn't room." The Tapoi thread closes. Dream 4:
the Sadhaba merchant pre-departure — monks and traders moved together,
it was practical. Minati to Gagan quietly: my grandmother called it letting
things go.

---

### Ch 15 — Return
*Location: Jaugada; Berhampur*

The return drive south to Jaugada. A village elder near the compound —
third Kalinga Jina mention, something his grandfather said once, doesn't know
what it was. Nabin tells Aman what the last sheet said: Pitambara's entry
for 1 April 1936. Padmini tells Aman about Satyabadi — the Gaan Karan of
Tentulia, the Arya Samaji who wrote about Bhagirath, who died of leprosy
untouched, famous and learned and uncelebrated. Aman by the Rushikulya: the
final notebook entry. Not a conclusion. A better set of questions.

---

### Ch 16 — Flight
*Location: Berhampur; Bhubaneswar airport; the flight*

The morning departure. Nabin's white Maruti in the courtyard. Padmini at the
door. The drive to Bhubaneswar. The flight home. Aman does not read on the
flight. The notebook is in his bag. He looks out the window. The last line
of the book is in the prose, not in the notebook — something small and
specific, not trying to close anything.
