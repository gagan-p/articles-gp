## Leg-by-Leg Journey

### Leg 1: Berhampur → Pandia/Jaugada → Bada Agula
**Chapters**: 1, 2
**Distance**: Berhampur → Pandia (~30km via Purushottampur) → Bada Agula
(~45km from Berhampur, ~25km from Pandia)
**Vehicle**: Gagan's rented car
**Day**: Day trip to Jaugada, drive to Bada Agula for overnight

**Route notes**: The drive from Berhampur to Pandia passes through
Purushottampur — a small town that was itself a minor administrative node.
The road from Pandia to Bada Agula enters the old Athagada zamindari country.
The villages get quieter. The roads get narrower.

**Night**: Bada Agula, the ancestral concrete house.

---

### Leg 2: Bada Agula — Two Nights
**Chapters**: 3, 4
**Day 1**: Drive back to Jaugada for Achintya's analysis (Ch 3). Return
to Bada Agula.
**Day 2**: The village day — no driving. The Alekh Akhada, Sona ma,
the Jatra.

Nabin arrives on Day 2 in the white Maruti. He will return to Berhampur
and come back for specific later legs.

**Night**: Bada Agula.

---

### Leg 3: Bada Agula → Athagada → (return or continue)
**Chapter**: 5
**Distance**: Bada Agula → Kabisuryanagar → Athagada (~35-40km southwest)
**Vehicle**: Gagan's car

**Route notes**: The road climbs into the low eastern edge of the ghats.
Paddy gives way to cashew orchards. The road narrows. The hills close in.
The Jagannath temple sits against a rock face at Athagada.

**Night**: Bada Agula or Berhampur depending on timing.

---

### Leg 4: → Bhanjanagar
**Chapter**: 6
**Distance**: From Athagada or Bada Agula → Bhanjanagar (~50-60km,
moving west up the Rushikulya valley)
**Who joins**: Minati's parents are here; they may follow the next leg
partway

**Route notes**: Moving with the grain of the terrain — toward the river.
Bhanjanagar is at the edge of the Ghumusar subdivision. The Rushikulya
is here at volume — post-monsoon, wide, cold, fast.

**Night**: Bhanjanagar, Minati's parents' house. On the Rushikulya.

---

### Leg 5: Bhanjanagar → Rushimala → Rest House
**Chapter**: 7, 8
**Distance**: Bhanjanagar → up the Rushikulya valley → road fails at
~6km from the spring → walk
**Who joins**: Minati's parents follow partway in their own car, then turn back

**Route notes**: The road follows the river upstream. The valley narrows.
The road fails — they park and walk. Old sal forest. The path becoming a
track becoming a direction.

**Night**: Forest Department rest house near Surada (after the walk back
down in the dark).

---

### Leg 6: Rest House → Chatrapur → Palur → Potagarh
**Chapters**: 9, 10
**Distance**: Rest house → Berhampur (refuel, brief) → Chatrapur → Palur
→ Potagarh coast

**Phone call**: Achintya by phone on the drive down — the theory stated
in full, the four weaknesses.

**Chatrapur**: District headquarters. Brief stop. Third Collectorate location.

**Palur**: The OIMSEAS excavation site. Hilltop stupa visible first.
Prasanna meets them at the site.

**Potagarh**: Two kilometres south of Palur along the coast road.
The buried fort, the old Masjid, the caretaker's shelf.

**Night**: Gopalpur or coast guesthouse.

---

### Leg 7: Coast → Brahmapur
**Chapter**: 11
**Distance**: Potagarh beach → Brahmapur/Berhampur (~20-25km)

**Net man**: On the Potagarh beach before leaving.

**Subrat's apartment, Brahmapur**: The tin trunk. Pitambara's papers.

**Night**: Nabin's house, Berhampur. Nabin joins.

---

### Leg 8: Berhampur → Dhauli
**Chapter**: 12
**Distance**: Berhampur → Dhauli, via NH16 north (~170km, approximately
3 hours)

**Route notes**: The NH16 is the old coastal highway connecting Madras to
Calcutta through Odisha. It passes through Brahmapur, Khallikote, Chilika
lake on the right, the Eastern Ghats receding on the left.

**Chilika**: Asia's largest coastal lagoon. In passing — the British railway
embankment changed its salinity; Irrawaddy dolphins still present.

**First time leaving Ganjam district.** Aman registers this.

**Night**: Bhubaneswar (hotel or family contact).

---

### Leg 9: Bhubaneswar cluster — Sisupalgarh → Khandagiri
**Chapter**: 13
**Distance**: All within 15km of central Bhubaneswar

**Route**: Dhauli → Sisupalgarh (~8km) → Khandagiri/Udayagiri (~6km)

**Night**: Bhubaneswar.

---

### Leg 10: Bhubaneswar → Cuttack (Bali Yatra)
**Chapter**: 14
**Date**: November 7th, Kartik Purnima
**Distance**: Bhubaneswar → Cuttack (~30km)

**Evening trip**: Drive at dusk. The Mahanadi at Cuttack. The ghats.

**Night**: Return to Bhubaneswar same night or stay in Cuttack.

Achintya departs for Assam from Bhubaneswar around this time. Available
by phone after this.

---

### Leg 11: Return to Jaugada
**Chapter**: 15
**Distance**: Bhubaneswar → Berhampur → Pandia (170km south)

Nabin drives separately in the white Maruti.

**At Jaugada**: The third visit. Nrusingha again, or a village elder.
The Pandava stones. The Rushikulya. The final notebook entry.

**Back in Berhampur**: Nabin's house. Padmini at the door.

---

### Leg 12: Departure
**Chapter**: 16
**Route**: Berhampur → Bhubaneswar airport (~170km) → KL (via Chennai
or direct)

Morning departure. The white Maruti.
